# All Fours Trinidad Rules

## Objective

All Fours is a trick taking card game played by four players in two teams of two.

Partners sit opposite each other. The goal is to be the first team to reach **14 points**.

## Players and Cards

* Players: 4
* Teams: 2 teams of 2
* Deck: Standard 52 card deck
* Card rank, highest to lowest: A, K, Q, J, 10, 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2
* Play direction: Anticlockwise starting with the person to the right of the dealer.

## Starting the Game

The deck is firstly shuffled, and cards are dealt randomly one by one face-up to each player. The first person dealt here does not matter. The first player to be dealt a Jack is declared the dealer.

The dealer shuffles, the player on the dealer’s left cuts the deck, and the dealer gives each player **6 cards**, usually in two rounds of 3 cards each.

After dealing, the dealer turns up the next card. This card is called the **kick** and its suit becomes the proposed trump suit. At this point, only the dealer and the person to the right of the dealer can watch their cards.

## Kicking Points

If the dealer kicks certain cards, the dealer’s team scores immediately.

### Trinidad Kicking Points

| Card Kicked | Points |
| ----------- | -----: |
| Ace         |      1 |
| 6           |      2 |
| Jack        |      3 |

### Tobago Variant

In Tobago, kicking the **2** gives 2 points in addition to the 6.

| Card Kicked | Points |
| ----------- | -----: |
| Ace         |      1 |
| 6           |      2 |
| 2           |      2 |
| Jack        |      3 |

## Begging

After the kick, the player to the dealer’s right decides whether to accept the trump suit.

They may say:

* **Stand**: Accept the trump suit and begin play.
* **I beg**: Ask for a different trump suit.

If the player begs, the dealer has two choices:

1. **Give one**
   The dealer keeps the original trump suit, and the opposing team gets 1 point.

2. **Run the cards**
   The dealer gives each player 3 more cards and turns up another kick card.

When running the cards:

* If the new kick is a different suit, that suit becomes trump.
* If the new kick is the same suit, the dealer deals 3 more cards again and kicks another card.
* The dealer’s team still scores any kicking points earned while running the cards.
* If the deck runs out before a new trump suit appears, the hand is usually thrown in and redealt. If points were kicked at any point, the dealer may "pass the pack" to the person to their right, making them the new dealer, or forgo the points and deal again. 

## Playing Tricks/Lifts

The player to the dealer’s right leads the first trick.

A trick/lift is won by:

1. The highest trump played, or
2. If no trump is played, the highest card of the suit that was led.

The winner of each trick/lift leads the next trick.

## Following Suit and Trumping

When a trump is played first:

* You must play a trump if you have one.
* If you have no trump, you may play any card.

When a non trump suit is led/called:

* If you have that suit, you must either follow suit or play a trump.
* If you do not have that suit, you may play any card.
* You are allowed to trump even if you can follow suit.

You cannot throw away a different non trump suit if you were able to follow the suit that was led.

### Undertrumping

If a non trump suit is led and a player has already played trump on that lift, later players may not play a lower trump than the highest trump already played.

The exception is a flush trump hand: if every card in your hand is trump, you may play any trump, even if it is lower.

## Trump and Follow Suit vs Trump and Anything

There are two variants to rules when playing trump if it is not led/called for that trick.

1. Trump and Follow Suit - This means that the person who played trump, if they won the lift, must start the next trick with the suit that was originally called.

2. Trump and Anything - This means that the person who played trump, if they won the lift, can star the new trick with any suit. 


## Scoring at the End of the Round

At the end of the round, teams score for **High, Low, Jack, and Game**.

| Point     | How It Is Scored                                | Value |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----: |
| High      | Team with the highest trump dealt               |     1 |
| Low       | Team originally dealt the lowest trump          |     1 |
| Jack      | Team that passes the Jack of trump safely       |     1 |
| Hang Jack | If the opposing team captures the Jack of trump |     3 |
| Game      | Team with the most card points in tricks        |     2 |

The first team to reach 14 of these points win. Points are awarded in the order listed. Therefore, for example, in a 13-13 game, the person who has High would win, since High is the highest priority.

## Game Point Card Values

Only these cards count toward the **Game** point. Note that game points are different from Round Points:

| Card                   | Value |
| ---------------------- | ----: |
| 10                     |    10 |
| Ace                    |     4 |
| King                   |     3 |
| Queen                  |     2 |
| Jack                   |     1 |
| 9, 8, 7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 2 |     0 |

Each team adds the value of the cards they won in tricks. The team with the higher total gets the 2 Game points.

If both teams tie for Game, the team that did not deal gets the point.

## Hang Jack

If the Jack of trump is captured by the opposing team, this is called **Hang Jack**.

Instead of scoring 1 point for Jack, the team that captures it scores **3 points**.

Example:

* Team A has the Jack of trump.
* Team B wins the trick containing that Jack.
* Team B scores 3 points for Hang Jack.

## Winning the Game

The first team to reach **14 points** wins.

When both teams are close to 14, points are counted in this order:

1. High
2. Low
3. Jack or Hang Jack
4. Game

This order matters because it decides who reaches 14 first.

## Common Terms

| Term          | Meaning                                                 |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Kick          | The card turned up by the dealer to decide trump        |
| Trump         | The strongest suit for the hand                         |
| Beg           | Asking the dealer to change the trump suit              |
| Take one      | Dealer keeps the trump suit and gives opponents 1 point |
| Run the cards | Dealer deals 3 more cards and kicks again               |
| High          | Highest trump dealt                                     |
| Low           | Lowest trump dealt                                      |
| Jack          | Jack of trump                                           |
| Hang Jack     | Capturing the opponent’s Jack of trump                  |
| Game          | Point for winning the most card value in tricks         |

## Quick Summary

1. Deal 6 cards to each player.
2. Dealer kicks a card to decide trump.
3. Dealer scores kicking points if they kick Ace, 6, or Jack in Trinidad.
4. In Tobago, dealer scores 2 points for kicking 2.
5. Player to dealer’s right may stand or beg.
6. Players play tricks anticlockwise.
7. Score High, Low, Jack or Hang Jack, and Game.
8. First team to 14 points wins.


## Common Calls During the Game

| Call          | Meaning                                                 |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Spin          | Play a low card that does not win the lift/trick.       |
| Go on Top     | Play a high card that can win the lift/trick            |
| Fatten        | Play a card that adds game points to the lift/trick.    |
| Shoot         | Play a high trump that can potentially hang a jack.     |
| Run with Jack | Safely play Jack to prevent it from getting hung.       |
